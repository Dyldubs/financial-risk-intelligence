SANCTIONS AND PEP SCREENING POLICY
Compliance Division | Policy Reference: COMP-003 | Version 1.8

1. OVERVIEW
This policy governs the screening of customers, counterparties, and transactions against sanctions lists and Politically Exposed Person (PEP) databases, as required under the AML/CTF Act 2006, the Charter of the United Nations Act 1945, and the Autonomous Sanctions Act 2011.

2. SANCTIONS SCREENING REQUIREMENTS
All transactions must be screened against:
- UNSC Consolidated Sanctions List (United Nations Security Council)
- OFAC SDN List (US Office of Foreign Assets Control — Specially Designated Nationals)
- Australian Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) consolidated list
- EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List

Transactions involving sanctioned entities must be blocked immediately and reported to the relevant authority within 24 hours.

3. HIGH-RISK COUNTRY CLASSIFICATIONS
The following country categories require enhanced transaction monitoring and additional due diligence:
- FATF High-Risk Jurisdictions Under Increased Monitoring (current list maintained by the Financial Action Task Force)
- Countries subject to Australian autonomous sanctions
- Correspondent banking risk: counterparty banks in jurisdictions with weak AML frameworks
- Offshore financial centres identified as high-risk by AUSTRAC guidance

4. TRANSACTION-LEVEL TRIGGERS
A transaction is flagged for sanctions review when:
- The originating or destination country is on the DFAT or OFAC list
- The transaction amount is material (>$1,000 AUD to/from high-risk jurisdiction)
- The customer's account has been linked to prior sanctions alerts
- Real-time geo-IP analysis indicates a high-risk jurisdiction

5. PEP SCREENING
Politically Exposed Persons (foreign and domestic) are subject to enhanced due diligence:
- Identity verified against commercially available PEP databases (World-Check, Dow Jones)
- Source of funds documented for transactions exceeding $5,000 AUD
- Annual re-screening required for all PEP-classified customers
- Senior Management approval required before establishing a relationship with a PEP

6. BREACH PROCEDURES
In the event of a confirmed sanctions match:
1. Block the transaction immediately
2. Do not alert the customer (tipping-off prohibition under s123 AML/CTF Act)
3. Notify the Compliance team within 1 hour
4. Submit a Suspicious Matter Report (SMR) to AUSTRAC within 24 hours
5. Freeze the account pending further investigation
