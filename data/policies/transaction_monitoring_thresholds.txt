TRANSACTION MONITORING THRESHOLDS AND RULES
Risk Operations | Rule Set Version 4.0 | Last Updated: March 2025

1. AMOUNT-BASED RULES
Rule TM-001: Cash Transaction Reporting (CTR)
  - Trigger: Single cash transaction >= $10,000 AUD
  - Action: Automatic TTR filed with AUSTRAC within 10 business days
  - Override: None — mandatory reporting regardless of customer risk rating

Rule TM-002: High-Value Electronic Transfer
  - Trigger: International wire transfer >= $5,000 AUD
  - Action: Enhanced due diligence; source of funds documented for HIGH/CRITICAL accounts

Rule TM-003: Structuring Alert (Smurfing Detection)
  - Trigger: 3 or more transactions between $8,000–$9,999 within a 48-hour window
  - Rationale: Pattern consistent with deliberate avoidance of CTR thresholds
  - Action: Immediate review by Financial Crime analyst; SMR if suspicion confirmed

Rule TM-004: Unusual Amount Relative to History
  - Trigger: Transaction amount > 5x the customer's 90-day average transaction value
  - Action: Flag for analyst review; auto-escalate to HIGH tier if also flagged for velocity

2. VELOCITY-BASED RULES
Rule TM-010: High Frequency — Short Window
  - Trigger: >= 4 transactions from same account within any 60-minute window
  - Action: MEDIUM tier minimum; transaction hold on 5th transaction pending review

Rule TM-011: High Frequency — Daily
  - Trigger: >= 15 transactions in a 24-hour period (excluding recurring direct debits)
  - Action: Review queue; customer outreach if pattern persists more than 3 days

Rule TM-012: Rapid Drawdown
  - Trigger: Account balance reduced by >80% within 72 hours of a large credit
  - Action: Funds hold and Financial Crime review

3. TIME-BASED RULES
Rule TM-020: Overnight Transactions
  - Trigger: Transaction processed between 11:00pm and 05:00am local time
  - Risk weighting: +0.1 added to composite risk score
  - Rationale: Correlated with account takeover and unauthorised access

Rule TM-021: After-Hours High Value
  - Trigger: Transaction > $2,000 AUD processed between 11:00pm and 05:00am
  - Action: Additional authentication check or analyst review the following morning

4. GEOGRAPHIC RISK RULES
Rule TM-030: High-Risk Country Flag
  - Trigger: Transaction origin or destination flagged as high-risk jurisdiction
  - Action: Automatic escalation to at least MEDIUM tier; HIGH if also velocity-triggered

Rule TM-031: Jurisdiction Change Alert
  - Trigger: Transaction from a country not seen in customer's last 12 months of history
  - Action: Step-up authentication; risk score increase

5. COMPOSITE ESCALATION RULES
Rule TM-050: Multi-Factor Escalation
  - Trigger: Transaction meets 3 or more of the above individual rules simultaneously
  - Action: Automatic CRITICAL tier regardless of individual rule severity
  - Rationale: Convergence of multiple risk indicators dramatically increases true positive rate

Rule TM-051: Repeat Flagging
  - Trigger: Account has been flagged more than twice in the past 30 days
  - Action: Account-level review; consideration of enhanced due diligence status
