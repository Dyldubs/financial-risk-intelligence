FRAUD TYPOLOGIES AND INDICATORS
Financial Crime Intelligence Unit | Reference Document v2.1

1. VELOCITY FRAUD
Description: Fraudsters rapidly make multiple transactions in a short window before the account owner or the bank detects the compromise.
Indicators:
- More than 3 transactions within a 1-hour window from a single card or account
- More than 15 transactions within 24 hours deviating significantly from historical baseline
- Rapid sequential transactions at different merchants with no plausible travel pattern
- Transactions immediately following a password reset or contact detail change

2. ACCOUNT TAKEOVER (ATO)
Description: A fraudster gains access to a legitimate customer's account and makes unauthorised transactions.
Indicators:
- Transaction at an unusual time of day (particularly 11pm–5am local time)
- Transaction from a new device or IP geolocation not seen in account history
- High-value transaction immediately following login from a new location
- Amount significantly exceeds the account holder's typical spend pattern

3. CARD-NOT-PRESENT (CNP) FRAUD
Description: Fraudulent use of card details for online or phone purchases where the physical card is not required.
Indicators:
- Multiple failed transactions (incorrect CVV, address mismatch) followed by a successful one
- Transaction at a high-risk merchant category (electronics, gift cards, digital currency)
- Billing address does not match registered account address
- Transaction originates from a proxy IP, VPN, or TOR exit node

4. FIRST-PARTY FRAUD
Description: A customer intentionally misrepresents their identity or circumstances to obtain financial benefit.
Indicators:
- Account opened with synthetic identity (new address, no credit history, funded immediately)
- High transaction volume shortly after account opening (within 30 days)
- Rapid drawdown of credit followed by default
- Multiple accounts sharing the same device fingerprint or phone number

5. MERCHANT FRAUD
Description: A merchant processes fraudulent transactions, often in collusion with cardholders.
Indicators:
- Merchant category code (MCC) inconsistent with expected business type
- High chargebacks relative to transaction volume
- Transactions processed outside normal business hours for the merchant type
- Unusually high average transaction value for the merchant category
